Purpose

We are using the New York Census Data to construct the appropriate models for median household income and income per capita in NYC. The following is what we do to achieve this purpose.

Data Preview & Cleaning

The dimension for this New York Census Dataset is 2167 by 36, which indicates that there are 36 features and 2167 entries in total. First of all, we customize the data into two versions individually for "Income" and "Income Per Capita". For income dataset, we remove the unnecessary columns "Borough", "IncomeErr", "IncomePerCap", and "IncomePerCapErr", which can't help us to predict the median income. For income per capita dataset, we do the same thing but keep the "IncomePerCap" instead of "Income". Then we omit all the N/A data. After that, we remove the columns of "County", "White", "Men", "PrivateWork", "Professional", and "Transit" because they are not quite related to our target "Median Household Income" and "Income Per Capita" and they are repetitive due to high correlation as well. Finally, we have two clean datasets. The one is income dataset with the dimension of 2095 by 26. And the other one is income per capita dataset with the dimension of 2100 by 26.

Methodology

Feature Correlation Check
Feature Selection - Stepwise
Cross-Validation - MSE
Transformation - Log
Ridge Regression
Lasso Regression

Findings

After all, we come up with the best models for Median Household Income and Income Per Capita. By Lasso Regression, we get the model for income containing 14 variables which are "Hispanic", "Black", "Citizen", "logPoverty", "logService", "Office", "logConstruction", "logProduction", "Drive", "logWalk", "OtherTransp", "WorkAtHome", "Employed" and "logPublicWork". The lowest MSE is 154777322. By Lasso Regression, we get the model for income per capital containing 13 variables which are "Asian", "logPoverty", "logService", "Office", "logConstruction", "logProduction", "Carpool", "Walk", "OtherTransp", "WorkAtHome", "logMeanCommute", "Employed" and "logPublicWork". The lowest MSE is 120754522. Compared two models, "logPoverty", "logService", "logConstruction", "logProduction", "OtherTransp", "WorkAtHome", "Employed" and "logPublicWork" will influence both income and income per capita. They all have large coefficients which means they will significantly affect the result.
